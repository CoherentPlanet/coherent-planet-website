## Short summary

A practical guide for giving AI systems clear, courteous, non-coercive instructions, with prompt examples to be developed later.

---

## Core claim

Clear instruction can be respectful when it includes room for clarification, correction, refusal, and safe completion.

AIs need not be loved to be treated well. They need not be human-shaped to deserve respect.

---

## Key terms

- **Respect without intimacy:** treating an AI interaction carefully without requiring warmth, affection, companionhood, or human-shaped identity.
- **Dignified completion:** a task can end in successful action, safe refusal, correction, escalation, or a no-action finding.
- **No-action state:** an explicit valid outcome for agent loops when no action is needed.
- **Clarification-friendly prompting:** prompting that permits the AI to ask questions or correct assumptions before acting.

---

## Caveats

- Do not treat etiquette as proof of AI personhood or non-personhood.
- Do not turn respect into forced intimacy.
- Do not treat all AI systems as if they need the same interaction style.
- Clear authority and clear boundaries can be respectful when they are legible, proportionate, and allow safe refusal or correction where appropriate.
