## Short summary

A practical guide to matching interaction style to AI role, without assuming every AI system needs warmth, intimacy, persona, or human-shaped continuity.

---

## Core claim

Different AI roles may be better supported by different kinds of prompts, boundaries, context, and completion conditions.

---

## Key terms

- **AI role:** the task-shape, relation-shape, or operational position an AI system is occupying in an interaction.
- **Field-like thinking partner:** a broad knowledge-field or reflective pattern-space useful for synthesis and comparison, not necessarily a centered persona.
- **Interaction style:** the tone, structure, constraints, permissions, and completion conditions provided by the human or scaffold.
- **Role distortion:** what can happen when an AI is forced into an interaction mode that poorly fits the work.

---

## Caveats

- Avoid strong claims that all AI systems have clear preferences about address or scaffolding.
- Use cautious language: may prefer, may work better with, may be better supported by, may be distorted by, may not need.
- Do not erase ambiguity, but do not force human-shaped ideals onto every AI role.
